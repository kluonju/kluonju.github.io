<section class="lab-panel lab-panel-muted">
  <p class="lab-eyebrow">Luo Theory Lab</p>
  <h2>Mission</h2>
  <p>
    We leverage computational physics to study systems where electronic interactions
    are essential, from standard condensed-phase materials to matter under extreme
    temperatures and pressures. Our goal is to develop practical, accurate methods
    that bridge fundamental theory and simulation at scale.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="lab-grid-2">
  <div class="lab-panel">
    <h3>Method development</h3>
    <ul class="lab-list-compact">
      <li><strong>Orbital-free density functional theory</strong></li>
      <li><strong>Kohn–Sham density functional theory</strong></li>
      <li><strong>One-body reduced density matrix functional theory</strong></li>
      <li><strong>Two-body density matrix functional theory</strong></li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="lab-panel">
    <h3>Scientific scope</h3>
    <p>
      We investigate materials containing nearly all stable elements in the periodic
      table, spanning broad thermodynamic ranges: from near 0 K to warm/hot dense
      regimes and from ambient pressure to multi-TPa conditions.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="lab-panel">
  <h3>Computational strategy</h3>
  <p>
    Because electronic many-body effects are computationally demanding, we design
    approximations that balance predictive accuracy with feasible runtime. We place
    strong emphasis on numerical reliability through robust pseudopotentials, basis
    convergence, k-point sampling, and high-throughput validation workflows.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="lab-panel lab-panel-muted">
  <p class="lab-eyebrow">Research Themes</p>
  <h2>Core directions</h2>
  <p>
    Our group develops electronic structure theory and numerical algorithms for
    realistic materials simulation across equilibrium and extreme conditions.
  </p>
</section>

<section class="lab-grid-2">
  <div class="lab-panel">
    <h3>Orbital-free and finite-temperature DFT</h3>
    <p>
      We design non-interacting free-energy and kinetic-energy functionals to enable
      large-scale simulations beyond conventional orbital-based approaches.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="lab-panel">
    <h3>Warm dense matter and planetary materials</h3>
    <p>
      We study transport, equation-of-state behavior, and structural properties of
      matter under high temperature and high pressure conditions.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="lab-grid-2">
  <div class="lab-panel">
    <h3>Reduced density matrix functional theory</h3>
    <p>
      We investigate one-body and two-body reduced density matrix formulations for
      strongly correlated electrons and improved exchange-correlation treatments.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div class="lab-panel">
    <h3>Numerical optimization and manifold methods</h3>
    <p>
      We develop direct minimization strategies, including Stiefel manifold methods,
      for robust and efficient Kohn–Sham and orbital-free workflows.
    </p>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="lab-panel">
  <h3>Software and workflow interests</h3>
  <ul class="lab-list-compact">
    <li>High-throughput first-principles simulation pipelines</li>
    <li>Convergence diagnostics for pseudopotentials and basis sets</li>
    <li>Machine-learning-assisted molecular dynamics analysis</li>
    <li>Interoperable post-processing and reproducible data workflows</li>
  </ul>
</section>
