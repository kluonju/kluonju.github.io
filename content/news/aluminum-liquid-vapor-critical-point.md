<p>
  The critical temperature, density, and pressure of liquid aluminum at its liquid–vapor
  critical point have been uncertain for decades, with reported <em>T</em><sub>c</sub> spanning
  thousands of kelvin. That spread matters for modeling laser ablation, release from shock,
  and other high-energy-density scenarios where the coexistence dome controls phase separation
  and transport.
</p>

<p>
  Here, <strong>deep-potential molecular dynamics</strong> trained on high-fidelity electronic-structure
  data is combined with spinodal analysis of the equation of state and direct coexistence
  simulations (with Gaussian-mixture phase identification). Benchmarking several
  exchange–correlation functionals against experimental liquid densities favors PBEsol for
  consistency. The analysis converges to a narrow window for
  <em>T</em><sub>c</sub>, <em>ρ</em><sub>c</sub>, and <em>P</em><sub>c</sub>, with uncertainties
  far smaller than in earlier estimates—a template for critical phenomena in other metals.
</p>

<ul class="lab-list-compact">
  <li>Resolves a long-standing spread in reported aluminum critical temperatures.</li>
  <li>Combines machine-learned potentials with coexistence and spinodal diagnostics.</li>
  <li>Benchmarks XC functionals against liquid-density data; transferable workflow.</li>
</ul>
